La Casita

A Baja retreat
unlike any other

Set on an expansive estate where ancient cardon cactus stand sentinel and bougainvillea blazes against white stucco walls, Mesa del Mar is the Baja that travelers dream about but rarely find.

Three spacious bedrooms, three bathrooms, a covered palapa terrace with hammock — plus shared access to the main estate's breathtaking infinity pool overlooking the Sea of Cortez and the Sierra de la Laguna mountains.

The kind of silence that reminds you how far you are from ordinary life. Fall asleep to desert breezes and wake to skies that held a million stars the night before.

The property features a stunning circular drive with a stone fountain and sweeping desert views toward the mountains of Baja.

The Full Estate

Your private
paradise awaits

The casita is the guest house of the main estate, and guests enjoy shared access to the property's crown jewel — a stunning infinity pool perched above the Sea of Cortez. Watch the water disappear into the horizon by day and surrender to some of Baja's most legendary sunsets by night.

The pool terrace offers panoramic views of the rugged Sierra de la Laguna mountains in one direction and the shimmering Sea of Cortez in the other. Lounge chairs, an umbrella, and the unhurried pace of Baja complete the picture.

As dusk falls, the sky transforms into a canvas of violet, gold, and coral — reflected perfectly in the still water of the pool. There is simply nowhere else you'd rather be.

Meet El Jefe

El Jefe is a classic Luhrs sport fisher built for the legendary waters of the Sea of Cortez — one of the world's great fishing grounds. Whether you're chasing marlin, dorado, or yellowfin tuna, or simply wanting to explore hidden coves and pristine coastline, El Jefe delivers the full Baja experience.

Fully equipped and crewed, she's available exclusively to Mesa del Mar guests. Combine a stay at the casita with a day on the water for the ultimate Baja adventure.

Getting Here

Find us in Baja

Mesa del Mar sits on the East Cape of Baja California Sur — a stretch of coastline that remains one of Mexico's last great wilderness escapes. Close enough to civilization for convenience, remote enough to feel like you've discovered it yourself.
